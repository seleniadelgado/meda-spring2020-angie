Solo Travel Tips to Get Started

A Guide on How to Solo Travel

Hello there, my name is Angie Delgado and I love to solo travel. It’s a concept not many women are familiar with because we do not only face scrutiny for it but also a lot of dangers. However, it is one of the most freeing and fulfilling experiences. If you are an introvert like me, yet enjoy the feeling of being somewhere you’ve never been before where no one knows you and you don’t know anyone then I recommend taking up solo traveling. I got into it because no one ever wanted to travel. I felt that I was pushing dates further and further out because I was always waiting for someone to join me. Turns out, I was missing out on precious time I could have spent planning and best of all travelling and meeting new people and learning of new cultures worse yet, I was missing out on a lot of foreign foods. So I took up the courage and moved to Argentina.


My seven steps for solo travel research:

A lot of my advice comes from my own experience and having travelled to 15 different countries. As I have travelled, I have also learned that I can not possibly make every mistake so I learn from other people's mistakes. Thank God for technology and access to other people's experiences, I have learned from using pages like yelp, tripadvisor and google to read reviews of accomodations and restaurants before signing my money away. Another very important, if not the most important way I have learned so much about travel is through Facebook groups, such as Women Who Travel, The Solo Female Traveling Network and Girls who love travel. I was blessed with being bilingual so I also get a lot of tips from Spanish groups, my favorite these days is Viajeros - Mochileros. These pages are filled with tips, photos, and best of all no matter how obscure your travel plans are, there's someone who has already done it and can give you insider scoop. These are people from all over the world who share their love for travelling with the world. Some of my favorite instagrammers to follow are @detantoandarviajes an Argentine radio station for travelling, @relatosenmochila, and someone who I actually met in person and appreciate a lot for what he taught me about photography @estebanmazzoncini who funds his travels with photos and by writing books of his experiences. Of course, my favorite instagram is my page and you can follow me at @angieselenia
